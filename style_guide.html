Process Analysis 1 - Red Trail Character Design

The Red Trail was a project for the animation course, IAT 343. We were tasked with going through the whole process of animating a short story in 3D.

As a team of one, I had to carefully scope my project. So I chose to make a short adaptation of the classic Red Riding Hood.

In order keep things within scope, I knew that working with simpler stylized shapes would be my friend. So going into designing the main character, Red, her shape design was my main focus. Consdering she needed to look young and innocent, in my intitial concept sketches, I tried to accentuate the bigger solid shapes (large boots and round cape) and to make sure she was mostly made of very rounded features.

In the final design, those design features stayed true. From her hood, the opening for her face is round to further make her face look rounder and the blush texturing on her face was used to make her cheeks and nose round.

Process Analysis 2 - Satelite Damage Ship Design

For the VR game Satelite Damage, I was in charge of designing the ship exterior.

I started off with exploring different ship shapes. Designing sci-Fi ships is always interesting as the designs can really emphasize interesting and unusual shapes. However, I had one particular constraint. The interior layout of the ship had already been layed out by our team member in charge of level design so however I design the ship, it needed to fit the interior.
